    • General Tips

    Sunlight

    In general, most houseplants should get about 5-6 hours of daylight
    per day. But remember: this fluctuates based on the plant.

    Typically, most plants thrive in bright & indirect sunlight. You should always
    do your research on what the best type of sunlight your plant thrives in.
    This will influence where you place your plant in your living space.


    Water

    Above all, you want to avoid overwatering your plants.
    A typical watering schedule could include every 1-2 weeks depending on the plant.

    However, some plants are tropical & like humidity and more
    water than other plants. Remember: Drainage holes are preferred, but more
    experienced plant owners may find themselves able to use modified
    watering schedules for plants with no drainage.


    Fertilizing

    Finding the right fertilizer & fertilizing schedule is important. Using the
    right fertilizer can encourage exponential growth in your plants.

    Beginners may find it useful to use baseline, catch-all fertilizer for generic houseplants.
    Typically, fertilizing should be done in the Spring and not in the Winter.
